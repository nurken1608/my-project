About Me

Albert Einstein

ETH Zurich: Diploma for Teaching Mathematics and Physics (1900); Doctor of Philosophy, University of Zurich (1905).

He's theoretical physicist who developed the theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics. He's work also includes significant contributions to quantum theory, notably the explanation of the photoelectric effect, for which he was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921.

Hobbies: Playing the Violin, Sailing, Philosophy, Pondering the nature of light and gravity, Correspondence with colleagues.

He's famous formula E=mc^2 continues to be a cornerstone of nuclear physics. He is known not only for my scientific achievements but also for his humanitarian views and commitment to peace and social justice.
